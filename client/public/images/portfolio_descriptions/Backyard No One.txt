I was still living in the mid-west at the time.  It was probably one of the smallest apartments I had ever lived it.  Zionsville, Indiana, home of the Bentley if I do recall.  The apartment complex was nestled against a State funded Preserve.  It was one early morning, the snow had just covered the grounds about.  As I slowly awakened from my slumber, stumbled into the living room, I had noticed the wonderous beauty Mother Nature had bestowed upon us.  The view was through my living room sliding glass door but what I had envisioned was a portal into the Winter Wonder Land about.

This was a quick piece, one that I immensely enjoyed.  It was my first winter in that location.  Such a peaceful area.  Having only lived in Zionsville for a couple of years, I still reminisce in how beautiful that moment truly was.  

Long term the idea was to turn it into what appeared as a Window Frame looking out into the world, and then add a base with a coat rack.  Never got around to the coat rack.  There's still time.
