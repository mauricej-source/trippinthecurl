Another Bob Ross inspiration that proved to be a labor of love, with intermittent cussing of course.

Bob Ross's palette knife work always fascinated me.  His level of expertise and ease of application, in addition to mixing colors and mediums in real-time was uncanny and magical.

Having never attempted such a technique, I decided to throw myself into it.  What did I have to lose I thought.  It would be another learning experience and if I enjoyed it, I would then have added another technique to my toolkit.

This beautiful snow covered mountain scene is actually Moraine Lake, in Banff National Park, Alberta Canada.  Someday I will make my way to this profound beauty.  I have seen multiple images where the water is so clear, you can see the bottom of the lake.  Like so may pieces this particular beauty currently hangs amongst so many others.  Every-time I look at this piece I want to grab a rowboat and oars and just row to the center and dip my toes in the mirrored waters

Someday soon.
