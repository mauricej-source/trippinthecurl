Ahhh, this piece actually represents a rather large shift in my life.  I took on this piece about the time I went back to the University for a second Bachelors of Science Degree in Computer Science.  Mechanical Engineering just wasn't the field I thought it would be.  

With change also comes challenge.  Going back to the University, at Indiana State University, there in Terre Haute, Indiana, was probably the largest challenge I had taken on in my life, mostly because it is a journey I took on myself, of my own volition and accord.  

This piece was a different kind of challenge.  One that not only turned into an interesting piece, but allowed me to carry some of the techniques and lessons into many pieces to come.  This was the combination of a request by my mother, in addition to an attempt at "Pointillism".  It also represented my first attempt at using Inks with pencil work on top of Matte board.  

This, I am afraid, was a first and last attempt at "Pointillism".  I dreamed about this pieces for months afterwards.  Something about patiently and meticulously placing one pencil mark on a Matte board, over, and over, and over eventually finds its way into your subconscious mind.

With so many people desiring the original, in addition to my mother, I thought what better way to share the work with everyone, than through the distribution of prints.  Being its own learning curve, for I had never distributed prints.  I purchased my first flat-bed scanner and went for it.  

The piece now belongs to my first cousin on my father's side of the family.  As the years have progressed and our immediate family has diminished to a certain extent.  I rarely see her anymore, but wanted to make certain she had something from me, from that side of the family, if only to give her a story and allow her to reminisce. 

Material:  Pencil, Ink on Matte-board

Dimensions:  20"(w) x 16" (h) x 0.25"(d)
