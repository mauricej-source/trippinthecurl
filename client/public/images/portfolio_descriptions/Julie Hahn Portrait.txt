This is one of many favorites.  it was a commissioned piece that turn out so well I created a second for my own portfolio.  It was a year after I had graduated from Highschool.  My senior year, I participated in the Year Book Club.  There we worked as a team to write, create copy, create artwork, take photos only to assemble them and ship the prototype off to publishing and eventually distribution for that year.  I had a lot of fun in the Yearbook Club and did create several pieces of artwork, including the cover for the year 1989.  

Having such a large distribution, my name eventually got out to several as an artist.  So much so, that a very young gentleman, at the time, Jamie Littleton, approached me, requesting a commissioned piece of his girlfriend.  AKA, Julie Hahn.

I did not know Julie at the time.  To be honest, I never really knew her, nor were we in the same circle or groups.  Jamie did not have a good picture of her, yet insisted that I draw her portrait as a Birthday Gift.  He eventually convinced me to take on the work, so I shook his hand, and suggested that I would see what I could do.

I took it upon myself to knock on her families door, recanting the story, as to the request, and lack of photograph.  Her mother was extremely kind.  Insisted I come in, sit down, and offer me something to drink.  As we sat there, she found the idea delightful and was able to provide a Junior Year Highschool Yearbook Photo that I could work from.

This particular piece was fun.  It helped me to continue to break ground in mixing Pencil, Pastels, Inks all on Matte board.
