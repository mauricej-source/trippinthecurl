This particular piece was definitely a labor of love.  One that represented the start of a new journey.  It is a first of many attempts at trying to find my own style, my own painting technique.  It started off as if I was working through a cardio exercise.  Large swaths of Acrylic paint, and an upward angle, left to right.  Moving quickly as acrylic dry's much more rapidly than oils.  It definitely kept me on my toes, as one color blended into the next.  To stand back, the sky looks like it is on fire.    

My inspiration for this piece was actually New Years Day, 2023.  I had stayed in Saint Augustine overnight.  It was an extremely foggy January 1st.  Waking early and packing the truck, I started the short trek home back to Jacksonville.  It occurred to me that I wanted some good coffee so I headed into downtown Saint Augustine.  Crossing the Vilano Beach Bridge, the sun started to crest the horizon.  It was something I just could not resist capturing.  From that day forward I knew this would become a painting.

Presently, like so many of my current pieces, it resides within a climate controlled storage unit.  Bubble wrapped against the the elements.  It awaits the day that I unwrap it, as if it were the day of Christmas, only to hang it and so many others upon the wall.  How i miss this particular piece. 

Material: Acrylic on Museum Quality Wrap Canvas with black painted gallery style edges 

Dimensions:  24"(w) x 36" (h) x 1.5"(d)
