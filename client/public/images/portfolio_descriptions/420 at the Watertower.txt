This is a well known Chicago Landmark, the Water Tower.  It has always been one of my favorite Chicago sites.   To walk up to it, to sit in front of it and enjoy its ornate beauty is timeless.  

A little history behind the Water Tower.

"Built in 1869 by architect William W. Boyington, the Chicago Water Tower was designed to house a 138-foot standpipe that regulated water pressure for the city’s new lake-intake system.  Constructed from yellow Joliet limestone in a Castellated Gothic Revival style, it is the second-oldest water tower in the United States, preceded only by the Louisville Water Tower. 

The tower gained iconic status as one of the few structures to survive the Great Chicago Fire of 1871, largely because firefighter Frank Trautman covered it in water-soaked woolen blankets to protect it from cinders.  Although it became functionally obsolete in 1906 and faced multiple demolition threats, public outcry preserved the landmark, which was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1971. 

Today, the 182.5-foot structure at 806 North Michigan Avenue no longer serves as a water facility; instead, it houses the City Gallery, an art gallery showcasing local photographers and filmmakers."
