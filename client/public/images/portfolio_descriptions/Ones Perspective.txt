This started out as a vacation to Moab, Utah, the Mountain Biking Mecca of the nation.  At least according to Paul Schumaker, at the time. 

We trekked west to Moab, Utah to do some Mountain Biking, one of Paul's passions.  I went to try my hand at it, in addition to just hanging out at the National Parks and snapping some photography.  Back then I was still using a Canon EOS Rebel model, but it was a 32mm wind.  I miss that camera, something about taking pictures and winding to the next shot, was simply therapeutic.  Anyway, I was shooting some Black and Whites, while Paul was out biking the "Slick Rock" Course.

I enjoyed this particular photo so much that I started a pencil drawing of it shortly thereafter. Having only finished half of it, I hence put it away for a later day. There it sat for a decade until 2023. Having it sit for as long as it had, it gave me a new perspective on how to finish it. So I decided to dust it off and do just that.

I do enjoy seeing a project finally coming to fruition.
