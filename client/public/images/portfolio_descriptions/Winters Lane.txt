After years of wanting a traditional easel, I finally broke down and purchased an H-Frame Easel in 2021.  No sooner did I order it, that I waited with great anticipation as to its arrival.  The day it showed up at my apartment door was an exciting one.  The delivery man wasn't happy, but I know I was.

It took the better part of a day to assemble the Easel.  At first attempt I thought I was missing some pieces and hardware, but it was buried within the styrofoam within the encased cardboard box.   Successfully assembled, I immediately unwrapped the plastic from an empty white canvas and set it onto the Easel.  "What should my first painting be?", I asked in the silence of the hobby room.  There the empty canvas sat, week, after week, until finally I stepped in, through colors onto a canvas, and said, 'Enough of this, lets get this started and see what happens."  I love moments like this, true pure imagination took over.  It eventually proved to be my first major palette knife piece.  I let the shape of the palette knife determine the shape and depth of the landscape.

Hiking through the mountains to the other side. The hiker's senses are at ease yet heightened as they work their way through. Eventually a clearing presents itself. The sense of peace and confidence fills the soul as the lane presents the next opportunity. Does the hiker accept such a fate? We may never know, but the  moment is breathtaking.
