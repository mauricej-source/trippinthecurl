Having moved to the State of Florida in 2017, Hurricane seasons were a new thing.  The locals insisted that I required the appropriate level of food to survive three days to a week outside of a refrigerator, they insisted on a generator, and don't forget the flashlights and batteries.  I always said thank you and suggested that I would heed their warning.  Most Hurricanes that I have been through to date have been no more than Tropical Storms by the time they make it inland.  There has been one or two that was pretty strong which resulted in residential damages, but I have always been fortunate.  Knocking on wood and throwing salt over my shoulder as we speak.  

Regardless, as the 2020 Hurricane Season approached, I thought I would take a moment and reminisce about the prior year's Hurricane Season, 2019.  It was one of the stronger seasons and provided me the necessary inspiration to bring this particular piece to fruition.

Hurricane Dorian was to decimate Florida from bottom to top.  By the time it reached Jacksonville, Florida it remained approximately 100 miles off the east coast, before proceeding further north. It never made landfall, which was rather bizarre and had several meteorologists puzzled, but there is sat, just spinning off shore, like a child's top.

Beautiful pale blue skies, bathed in deep water blues and aquamarine high-lights.  This was a wonderful piece to have had the opportunity to paint and is the original piece that eventually became the name of my website.  

Material: Oil on Museum Quality Wrap Canvas with black painted gallery style edges

Dimensions:  30"(w) x 24" (h) x 1.5"(d)
