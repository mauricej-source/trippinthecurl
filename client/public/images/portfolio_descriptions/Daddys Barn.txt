I have actually spoken to this piece in another artwork posting, 'Fathers Farm'.

This represents an second attempt at a Commissioned Piece.  One that an old high-school friend approached me on, years ago.  Her desire was a painting of one of her favorite childhood memories; her fathers old barn.  As you can imagine it took a little longer than originally anticipated as this was a second attempt.  To force me into an accurate representation and perspective I drew the image onto the canvas with a soft pencil prior to painting.  Rarely do I take this approach, only because I prefer the free flowing aspect of applying paint to canvas allowing my brain to find and determine the overall scope and breadth of such.

Needless so say, I eventually completed the commissioned piece, and happily delivered it to her, at the old Paris, Illinois McDonalds, prior to Christmas of 2019.

Material: 
Oil on Museum Quality Canvas with black painted gallery style edges

Dimensions:     
24"(w) x 12" (h) x 1.5"(d)
