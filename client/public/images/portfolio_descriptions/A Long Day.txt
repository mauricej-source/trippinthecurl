Bob Ross is one of many artists who have influence my painting style over the years.  I always found it fascinating at how quickly the man could produce a full painting in 25 minutes.  I have watched countless episodes of him walking through technique.  If you are not careful, he soothing voice will put you right to sleep on your favorite couch or easy chair.  It between his Happy Little Clouds and Trees, he introduced me to the "Wet on Wet" Technique.

I found myself compelled to try his repeated "Wet on Wet" Technique mostly due to what seemed like his effortless attempts at painting clouds and cloud cover.  It was absolutely mesmerizing.  I fore one struggled with painting clouds, and found the process painstakingly difficult, so what did I have to lose.

This piece represents my first of many attempts at Bob Ross' "Wet on Wet" Technique.  It is a continued practice of capturing everything beautiful about nature and the world about us.  

Entitled A Long Day -- Some days seem like they last for an eternity; whereas others pass with the blink of an eye.  My preference, of course, being the former. This was a wonderful piece to paint.  One of my favorite compliments was from my father.  'How in the world did he paint all of those trees?  Look at how tight they are.'  It still makes me smile thinking of the moment.
