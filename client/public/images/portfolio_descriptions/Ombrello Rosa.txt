The story behind this piece is interesting. This originally started at as an Acrylic Painting. While in Zionsville, Indiana, I attended a "Wine n Canvas" some know it as a "Paint n Sip". It was not my first time attending and to be honest most would approach me and tell me I was cheating or I did not belong in such classes, because of the quality of work. I would go anyway, because they were fun for me and helped me practice my paint times.
​
Needless to say, this piece was done at the height of what I referred to as the "Lonely Umbrella" Movement. Everywhere you looked there was a painting of a woman with an umbrella, alone, walking the path of life.

When the evening's class had come to a close, my initial was to take it home and touch it up in a few areas. Well just touching up turned into a full over-paint with Oils. My first over-paint :)

Needless to say this was a fun piece to paint and now hangs on a friend's wall.

Material:
Acrylic & Oil on Museum Quality Wrap Canvas with black painted gallery style edges
