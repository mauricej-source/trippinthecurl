It seems I am always on a pursuit to challenge myself.  Carving in Grey Linoleum proved successful the first time, with the 'Willow Tree'.  It started as one idea and turned into something completely different.  There has always been a third phase to the Grey Linoleum Pieces.  Hopefully that phase will come to fruition this year, 2026.  I would elude to it, but would not wish to detract from the current state of this artwork.

What follows is a second attempt at the Grey Linoleum Medium.  Something I had never imagined to find myself working in.  It has been a constant learning curve.  Compensating for texture, detail, depth, and lighting.  What proved to be most time consuming, was the determination on how the background blended with the mid-ground and foreground, how the snow flowed across the grounds, how snow pile upon the stone wall, how the wall's perspective slowly dwindles off into the distance.  the largest obstacle was the variance between the barn and the barren tree in front of it.  What I did not compensate for was the depth of the linoleum itself.  It did not provide enough to allow for a greater amount of depth, hence why it appears flat in some areas.  Like I say, learning curves.  Regardless, I enjoyed the variations and hey it looks awesome on a wall.

Material:  Grey Linoleum on Particle Board

Dimensions:  18"(w) x 12" (h) x 1.25"(d)
