I started this piece with a particular idea in mind.  It started its journey as a Commissioned Piece, one that I had promised an old high school friend of mine. 

As I continued to paint, I found myself beautifully enamored within the detail. The more detail I added the more my mind floated into bliss. I found myself painting trees, pasture, and grass without realizing what I was doing.  After a few hours it felt as if no sooner had I started, the sun had set across the horizon.  Day after day continued in this fashion.  I eventually found myself wondering why my feet hurt, turning to the clock, realizing that  5 sometimes 6 hours straight had lapsed. It is a beautiful world to find yourself engulfed by.  I love imagination.

Once completed, what started out as a commissioned piece, turned into a landscape similar to my grandfather, William Webster out in Elbridge Township, Illinois.  The barn was not quite identical, but the landscape, up and off to the right, as the field extends into the distance is very similar that I found myself walking to many years ago.  

As for my friend I promised her another, you may find it entitled 'Daddy's Barn'.  Once you compare the two you will see why I started anew.

Material:  Oil on Museum Quality Canvas with white gallery style edges

Dimensions:  24"(w) x 18" (h) x 0.75"(d)
