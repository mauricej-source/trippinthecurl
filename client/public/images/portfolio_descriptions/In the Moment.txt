When I started this piece, I knew it was meant for me and me alone.  It would not be for sale nor would it hang on another's wall.  At least in my lifetime.  This was a piece meant to challenge me, meant to force me to think outside of the box, as I applied different painting styles and contrasting colors.

So yes, it was meant to deliberately force myself out of my comfort zone.  How frustrating was it you ask?  Well I am confident that I had countless conversations questioning the 'Whys' and 'Hows'.

Different techniques were chosen for cloud cover, background, mid-ground, and foreground.  In addition, both contrasting colors and mid-ground were chosen as areas of focus.

The lesson I took away is that we must continuously strive to be a better person than the moment before.  By forcing myself to walk paths unknown are meant to sharpen the mind, to hone the skills necessary so that I can take on the next challenge, and the one after that.

Also it is worth noting, this is the first piece I have painted someone into the scenery.  That someone is me.  Seated upon the bench, admiring the landscape, and what had been created.  Of all of the paintings that I have done, it was my brother who remarked upon this particular piece on more than one occasion.  I had never let anyone know that it was I seated upon the bench, yet my brother knew immediately.  He recognized the back of my head by the huge swirling cowlick in my hairline.  This made me smile, because I spent countless hours looking at the back of my head, so it would look like me seated upon that very bench.  For my brother to have remarked on it though will always mean the world to me.  I think of him often
