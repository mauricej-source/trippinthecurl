My last, albeit favorite portrait.  

There may come a day where I might do another.  As of this moment, this particular piece retains the 'Last' Title.

It was definitely a labor of love only because I was head over heals for the woman.  Like so many portraits before, I worked deliberately and meticulously at this particular piece.  It was to be considered a gift and by god must be perfect.  

I recall needing an enlarged image of the photograph to get a better understanding of the detail within the portrait itself.  The dark shirt prevented me from finding the necessary shadow and depth desired.  There were also areas of the photo slightly blurred or who knows maybe aged to the point definition was worn away.  To be honest, I do not recall now.  I only recall that I needed enlarged copies to proceed.  At least in my own mind.

At the time, I was in Carbondale, Illinois and no one would allow me to enlarge the existing photo due to copyright infringement.  At the time all photographers and developers places there company's logo and signature on the back of photos.  Determined to enlarge the photograph, I thought how to get around this little conundrum.  Then it occurred to me that my favorite Student Union Bookstore sold Ink Erasures.   So I trotted off to purchase a 0.50 cent Ink Erasure.  

Wouldn't you know it, the company and photographer name no longer existed on the back of the photo.  Two days later I picked up the enlarged copies and happily finished the portrait.  

Ahhhhhhhh, fond memories.
