I have always enjoyed the Impressionistic, Neo-impressionistic era, Van Gogh being my favorite, Monet, Manet, Seurat there are countless.  That era has always been a motivator, to push myself, to drive towards a better version of me.  A better version of me, who can in turn produce a better version of art.  Sounds slightly egotistical I realize, but it has helped me to grow and change as a human being.  One I feel is better, one that I believe is better, and better of for such growth.

One of my first attempts at oil painting was Starry Night by Van Gogh.  Followed by my second, San Giorgio Maggiore at Twilight, by Claude Monet, my third attempt at oil painting was Autumn as Nuenen, by Van Gogh.  All of these pieces were eventually given to family members so I never really captured descent pictures of them to add to my portfolio.  I have San Giorgio, posted here.  My hope is that I will eventually capture the other two for prosperity's sake.  There is a little story about 'Starry Night' that I enjoy sharing, but not here.  At least not until I get a good picture of it to post.

How I truly enjoy painting the masters.

Hopefully i will get a better picture some day, the camera light bounced off of the painting.  To see it hanging in my parents den is a moment of pride for me.  They have had countless friends and colleagues both comment and compliment the pieces that hang in their house.  One in particular suggested the enjoyed how the light and colors reflect off of the river's surface.
