My goodness.  This piece actually started as a conversation at work.  I had moved to Florida back in 2017 and eventually found myself working at Allstate in Jacksonville, Florida.  There I met a fellow colleague who eventually became a fast friend, Dean Fudge.  

At the time we speaking about hobbies and what we enjoyed doing for fun.  He mentioned his many great adventures of scuba diving; whereas I spoke of my artwork.  He asked if I could paint him something, but it was paint on canvas he was seeking.  He wanted me to paint one of his aluminum of heavy metal diving tanks.  Something I had never done nor knew how to proceed.  I hesitated, suggesting that I had never done anything like that and would have to research it and get back with him.  As to the subject matter he wanted a shark or whale theme, possibly a varied array of fish and ocean theme.  What about the Lionfish I asked?  Eventually I found myself practicing and painted this piece.  Unfortunately it never found it's way to a diving tank, COVID shut down the industry and Dean Fudge eventually moved back to Canada.

Simply entitled Lion-fish, and they say Charlie Tuna, is the King of the Seas.  This is a colorful representation of a Lion-fish, lackadaisically swimming beneath the waters, amongst the beautiful coral reef.  This actually represents the first animal I have ever painted.  

Material: Oil on Museum Quality Canvas with black painted gallery style edges

Dimensions:  18"(w) x 24" (h) x 1.5"(d)
